This project is based on Assistive technology (AT). Assistive technology is any item, piece of equipment, software program, or product system that is used to increase, maintain, or improve the functional capabilities of individuals with disabilities. It helps people who have difficulty speaking, typing, writing, remembering, pointing, seeing, hearing, learning, walking, and several other conditions. Different disabilities require different assistive technologies. Assistive technology promotes greater independence by enabling people to perform tasks they were formerly unable to accomplish, or had great difficulty accomplishing, by providing enhancements to, or changing methods of interacting with the technology needed to accomplish such tasks. For example, wheelchairs provide independent mobility for those who cannot walk, while assistive eating devices can enable people who cannot feed themselves to do so. Due to assistive technology, people with disabilities have an opportunity of a more positive and easy-going lifestyle.

PURPOSE

The main purpose of this project is to allow people with disabilities to participate more fully in all aspects of life such as home, school, and their surrounding community and increases their opportunities for education, social interactions, and potential for meaningful employment. Quadriplegia is one of the cases where the patient is unable to move any part of his body below the neck. Complex and expensive interfaces are used for solving this issue. One effective solution for this condition is Tongue Drive Assistive System (TDAS). In TDAS, the tongue movement is an input to the processor. In previous solutions of TDAS, patients were necessitated to undergo surgery. In order to eliminate the process of surgery, this project presents a unique approach where tongue movements are sensed from externally connected wireless head band to control devices and to communicate with people. The proposed idea is more hygienic, simple and can perform a greater number of commands as compared to other existing assistive technology solutions.

Problem Statement and Solution

Quadriplegia and Spinal Cord Injuries makes a person impaired for life and leave him with an inability to carry out his usual daily tasks and lead a normal life. The tongue is connected to the brain by the hypoglossal cranial nerve which generally escapes injury and loss of motion after such accidents. It can move very fast and accurately. Furthermore, the tongue muscle is similar to the heart muscle. It is the strongest muscle in the body and does not fatigue easily. The Tongue Drive Assistive System sends the signals from the XY Axis joystick(Transmitter) to the Wheelchair(Reciever). The transmitter and reciever contains a set of specific user-defined commands that enables the movement of the wheelchair in the desired direction.

METHODOLOGY

The working principle of this project is simple and effective. In this project, a Head band is to be worn by the user. The headband will contain an XY-Axis Joystick closer to the mouth. As the individual pushes the joystick in specific directions with the help of his/her tongue, the joystick transmits the signals to the reciver(wheelchair). The commands from the headband are transmitted to the reciever via wireless systems. This is an Arduino project where the Reciever(Mega) drives the wheelchair in all directions and the Transmitter(Uno) sends signals and produces alarms in emergency situations via speakers that are attached to the wheel chair. The emergency button is located near the joystick and is easily accessible by the tongue. The system also has the feature of sensing the obstacles ahead and producing a beep sound that can help Partially blinded individuals.

According to WHO, every year, around the world, between 250,000 and 500,000 people suffer a spinal cord injury (SCI) and Quadriplegia. Due to high costs of treatment and unaffordability, a vast majority is left with the disability. They are not only bounded by wheelchairs but are greatly independent on care-takers and home-care facilities to carry out even minor tasks for themselves. Every wheelchair that is enabled with assistive technology are highly priced and unaffordable to most of the disabled people. The project aims at building an assistive wheelchair that is affordable to most of the disabled individuals and also includes multiple features that will help improve their conditions, reduce dependency and enhance quality of living.
